#### Jimmy is a computational researcher, architect, and urban designer whose work sits at the intersection of machine learning, smart cities, housing and collective living, and historic preservation. His current research focuses on (1) developing computational methods, including LLMs and machine learning, to visualize buildable deltas and anticipate future development potential in metropolitan regions, and (2) exploring neighborhood renewal strategies through data-informed analyses of urban adjacency conditions.
####
#### As the co-founder of Commons Office, a research-minded design practice, Jimmy embraces a trans-scalar and trans-disciplinary approach across architecture, urbanism, engineering, and artificial intelligence. The practice views design as a collective social agency that empowers the commons and advances the common good through critical inquiry, stakeholder collaboration, and the application of advanced computational methods.
####
#### Jimmy is currently an MS in Computational Science and Engineering student at Georgia Tech. He holds a Master in Architecture in Urban Design (MAUD) from Harvard University and a Bachelor of Architecture from Inner Mongolia University of Technology.
